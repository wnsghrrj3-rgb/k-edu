📖 지문 읽기 — Passage A
For decades, scientists believed that the brain stopped developing after childhood. However, recent research has revealed that the brain continues to change throughout a person's entire life — a phenomenon known as neuroplasticity. Learning a new language, picking up a musical instrument, or even changing daily routines can create new neural pathways. This means that it is never too late to learn something new or to recover from brain injuries.

📖 지문 읽기 — Passage B
When honeybees discover a food source, they return to the hive and perform a series of movements known as the "waggle dance." The direction and duration of the dance communicate the exact location and distance of the food to other bees. This remarkable behavior allows the colony to efficiently share information without any spoken language. Scientists continue to study this communication system as one of the most sophisticated forms of non-human signaling in nature.

📖 지문 읽기 — Passage C (고난도)
Urban planners have long focused on building wider roads to reduce traffic congestion. Paradoxically, this approach often makes the problem worse — a phenomenon called "induced demand." When roads are expanded, more people choose to drive, eventually filling the new lanes and returning congestion to its original levels. Cities that have instead invested in public transportation, cycling infrastructure, and walkable neighborhoods have seen more lasting improvements in traffic flow.

Most people assume that multitasking makes them more productive. In reality, the brain does not perform two complex tasks simultaneously — it switches rapidly between them. Each switch costs time and mental energy, leading to more errors and slower completion of both tasks. Studies consistently show that focusing on one task at a time leads to higher quality work and greater efficiency.
