The concept of "grit" — the combination of passion and perseverance — has become a popular topic in education. Psychologist Angela Duckworth argues that grit is a stronger predictor of success than talent or intelligence. Her research shows that individuals who maintain long-term goals and work consistently toward them tend to outperform their more naturally gifted peers. However, critics point out that grit alone cannot overcome systemic barriers such as poverty and discrimination.

독해 · 주제
The "bystander effect" describes the phenomenon where individuals are less likely to offer help in an emergency when other people are present. Psychologists Darley and Latané demonstrated this through experiments showing that as the number of bystanders increases, the probability of any single person intervening decreases. This occurs because responsibility becomes diffused among the group — each person assumes someone else will act. Understanding this psychological tendency is crucial for designing effective emergency response training.
