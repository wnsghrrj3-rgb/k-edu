True innovation requires the courage to ______. The greatest inventions in history — from the airplane to the internet — were initially dismissed as impossible or foolish. Those who changed the world were willing to risk failure and ridicule.
고난도: 추상 주제
Q2 ⭐⭐⭐⭐

다음 글의 주제로 가장 적절한 것은?

A map is not the territory it represents. Similarly, our mental models of reality are not reality itself. When we confuse our interpretations with facts, we close ourselves off to new information that might challenge our existing beliefs. The wisest approach is to hold our models loosely.

밑줄 친 부분이 의미하는 바는?

(A) Grit is defined as the combination of passion and perseverance toward long-term goals.
(B) Research suggests that grit is a better predictor of success than IQ or talent alone.
(C) This has led educators to explore ways of developing grit in students.
고난도: 문장삽입
Q6 ⭐⭐⭐⭐

주어진 문장이 들어갈 곳은?

"Yet this very efficiency can become a trap."
① Our brains are wired to form habits because they save mental energy. ② Once a behavior becomes automatic, we no longer need to think about it. ③ When harmful habits form, the same automatic process makes them extremely difficult to break. ④ Understanding this mechanism is the first step toward change.
고난도: 추상 제목
Q7 ⭐⭐⭐⭐

다음 글의 제목으로 가장 적절한 것은?

When we praise children for being "smart," they become afraid of challenges that might reveal they're not as smart as people think. But when we praise effort — "You worked really hard on that" — children develop resilience and a love of learning.

다음 글의 요지로 가장 적절한 것은?

We tend to think of failure as the opposite of success. But in reality, failure is often a necessary step on the path to success. Every mistake contains a lesson, and those who learn from their failures are more likely to achieve their goals than those who never try at all.
