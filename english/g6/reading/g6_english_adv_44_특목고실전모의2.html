The Dunning-Kruger effect reveals an ironic truth about human cognition: those with the least knowledge in a given area tend to overestimate their competence the most, while genuine experts often underestimate theirs. This occurs because incompetence robs people of the very skills needed to recognize their own limitations. Conversely, experts are so aware of how much they don't know that they become overly cautious in their self-assessments.

Mirror neurons, discovered in the 1990s, fire both when an individual performs an action and when they observe the same action performed by another. These neurons are believed to play a fundamental role in empathy, language acquisition, and social learning. When we watch someone smile, our mirror neurons activate the same neural pathways as if we ourselves were smiling, which may explain why emotions are contagious.
