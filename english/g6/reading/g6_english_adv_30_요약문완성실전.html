⚠️ 함정: 글에 나온 단어지만 요약문 맥락에 안 맞는 선택지!
📖 지문 A
When people set extremely ambitious goals, they often feel overwhelmed and end up procrastinating. Research suggests that breaking a large task into smaller, manageable steps makes it easier to get started and maintain motivation. Each small success creates a sense of achievement that fuels further progress. In this way, modest milestones can lead to extraordinary results over time.
→ Setting overly ambitious goals can cause (A), but dividing tasks into smaller steps helps maintain (B).

📖 지문 B
Many companies encourage competition among employees, believing it drives performance. However, excessive competition can undermine teamwork and create a hostile work environment. When colleagues see each other as rivals rather than partners, they become reluctant to share knowledge or help one another. Organizations that foster collaboration instead of rivalry tend to achieve higher overall productivity.
→ While companies promote (A) to boost performance, too much of it can damage (B) and reduce productivity.

📖 지문 C (고난도)
Parents often praise children by saying "You're so smart!" when they succeed. However, psychologist Carol Dweck found that this kind of praise can backfire. Children who are told they are smart tend to avoid challenges for fear of failing and losing their "smart" label. In contrast, children praised for their effort — "You worked really hard!" — are more likely to embrace difficult tasks and persist through setbacks.
→ Praising children's (A) rather than their effort can make them (B) challenges instead of embracing them.
